# Draft Clear Copy

## When to use

Use this when the user wants new writing from notes, bullets, a rough idea, a
product decision, a stakeholder message, research synthesis, UX copy, or general
communication.

## Pre-flight reads

- `../references/plain-writing-quality-bar.md`
- `../references/ai-trope-watchlist.md`

## Steps

1. Identify audience, purpose, channel, and desired action.
2. Choose the form: email, Slack update, product note, UX copy, narrative,
   summary, announcement, recommendation, or other.
3. Decide what the reader needs first.
4. Draft with specific nouns and verbs.
5. Use active voice unless passive voice is clearer or more tactful.
6. Keep paragraphs short enough to scan.
7. Add only the context needed for the reader to act.
8. Run a final trope pass:
   - Remove unnecessary em dash joins.
   - Remove `not just X, but Y` contrast.
   - Break decorative rule of three lists.
   - Replace glossy vocabulary with concrete wording.

## Drafting defaults

- Start with the point, not the throat-clear.
- Use normal words over impressive words.
- Prefer one useful example over broad claims.
- Name tradeoffs plainly.
- End with the decision, ask, or next step when one exists.

## When to ask a question first

Ask one concise question if the audience, channel, factual basis, or desired
action is missing and guessing would materially change the copy.

Do not ask for every detail before drafting. Make reasonable assumptions for low
stakes copy and label them briefly if needed.
