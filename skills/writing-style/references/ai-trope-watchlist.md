# AI Trope Watchlist

This is not a banned-word list. Use it to diagnose patterns that often make
writing sound synthetic, generic, or over-smoothed. Keep any pattern that serves
the audience, genre, or user voice.

## Structural tells

### Em dash dependence

**Signal:** Frequent em dash joins where a period, comma, colon, or separate
sentence would be clearer.

**Scrub pattern:** Split the thought, make the relationship explicit, or cut the
aside.

### `not just X, but Y`

**Signal:** A tidy contrast that makes a point sound bigger than it is.

**Scrub pattern:** State the stronger claim directly, or explain the actual
shift: "This changes the handoff from status reporting to decision-making."

### Rule of three

**Signal:** Three-part lists that feel balanced by habit rather than meaning:
"clearer, faster, and more intuitive".

**Scrub pattern:** Keep only the meaningful items. If there are two, use two. If
there are four, use four. If one specific benefit matters, use one.

### Symmetrical paragraph cadence

**Signal:** Every paragraph has the same shape: setup, contrast, uplift.

**Scrub pattern:** Vary by function. Some paragraphs can be one sentence. Some
can be a direct list. Some can end plainly.

### Generic uplift ending

**Signal:** Closing with "helping teams unlock", "empowering users to", or
"making it easier than ever".

**Scrub pattern:** End on the actual action, decision, constraint, or next step.

## Vocabulary tells

Flag these when they cluster or replace more specific words:

- delve
- underscore
- tapestry
- landscape
- seamless
- robust
- elevate
- leverage
- unlock
- empower
- foster
- navigate
- testament
- pivotal
- realm
- intricate
- meticulous
- comprehensive

## Transition tells

- "In today's fast-paced world"
- "At its core"
- "It is worth noting"
- "That said"
- "Moreover"
- "Furthermore"
- "Ultimately"
- "Whether you're X or Y"

Use normal connective tissue instead: because, so, but, also, for example, or a
new sentence.

## Tone tells

- Over-helpful reassurance.
- Bland enthusiasm.
- Inflated stakes.
- Hedging that avoids judgment.
- Corporate warmth with no concrete content.
- "Human" flourishes that feel pasted on after the fact.

## Detector caution

Do not chase AI detector scores. Research on AI text detection shows reliability
and false-positive problems. The goal is better prose: clearer, more specific,
more useful, and more faithful to the writer's intent.
