# UserTesting.com Platform Capabilities

Use this reference when drafting scripts or asking users what to export. It captures the platform realities the skill should assume unless the user supplies account-specific details.

## Planning primitives

A UserTesting.com study can include:

- Audience filters: broad targeting options available in the platform.
- Screener questions: custom qualification questions that include or exclude contributors.
- Starting URL or scenario: where the participant begins and what context they should imagine.
- Tasks: participant activities organized around a realistic goal.
- Task groups: related task sections when the study needs structure.
- Verbal response prompts: think-aloud or reflection prompts captured in session video and transcripts.
- Written response prompts: short typed answers when structured text is useful.
- Multiple choice questions: structured choices for simple categorization.
- Rating scales: numeric or labeled scales for confidence, ease, satisfaction, likelihood, or similar perceptions.
- Success and difficulty metrics: task-level measures that can help summarize whether participants completed a task and how hard it felt.
- Five Second Tests: short first-impression tasks for visual comprehension.
- Comparison tasks: side-by-side or sequential evaluation of alternatives when balanced wording and order are handled carefully.
- Camera tasks: participant-provided visuals when the research question requires context from their environment.

Use these blocks directly in the script instead of inventing generic survey formats.

## Audience and screeners

Audience definition should be part of the brief, not a late setup step. Capture:

- Target audience: who should take the test.
- Relevant behavior or experience: what they have done, used, bought, considered, or tried.
- Device or platform: desktop, mobile web, iOS, Android, tablet, or browser constraints.
- Geography, language, role, company type, accessibility needs, or other material constraints.
- Exclusions: people who should not qualify because they would distort the result.
- Segments: audience groups to compare, such as new vs returning users or novice vs expert users.

Prefer behavior-based screeners over demographic-only screeners. Use demographics when they materially affect the decision or recruiting feasibility.

Good audience definition:

```text
People in the UK who have compared broadband providers online in the last 6 months and are comfortable completing a desktop web test.
```

Weak audience definition:

```text
Adults aged 25-45.
```

## Export and handoff realities

For synthesis, ask for the UserTesting.com Excel export first when available. It can provide the most compact structured handoff and may include transcripts, participant demographics, written responses, multi-select responses, notes or clip metadata, and metrics depending on account features and study setup.

Also accept:

- transcript exports;
- copied session transcripts;
- clips or highlight summaries;
- notes or tagged observations;
- metrics screenshots or tables;
- participant/session lists;
- manually pasted session notes.

Ask the user to include these fields when possible:

- participant ID or anonymized label;
- audience segment;
- task name or number;
- success and difficulty metrics;
- rating-scale answers;
- notable quotes;
- observed behavior;
- clip links or timestamps;
- screener criteria.

## Platform-fit checks

Before a script is launch-ready, check:

- The study can be represented using UserTesting.com blocks.
- Each task has one primary goal.
- Follow-ups are mostly verbal responses.
- Written responses are short and intentional.
- Metrics are used only when the team will act on them.
- Screeners qualify the right people without revealing the target answer.
- The expected duration fits the study type.
