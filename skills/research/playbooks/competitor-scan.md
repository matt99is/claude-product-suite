# Competitor Scan

## When to use

Use this when the user wants to compare competitors, comparators, examples, or
market patterns against a UX/product question.

## What Claude needs first

- The product, category, or experience being compared.
- The competitor or comparator set, if known.
- The comparison dimensions.
- What the scan must inform.
- Any geography, sector, device, audience, or date constraints.

## Pre-flight reads

- `../references/source-quality.md`
- `../references/research-pitfalls.md`

## Steps

1. Confirm the brief and what the scan must inform.
2. Define comparison dimensions before collecting examples.
3. Identify competitors, comparators, or example products.
4. Prefer direct product/account evidence where possible.
5. Separate direct product evidence from commentary and vendor claims.
6. Classify source quality before synthesis.
7. Compare patterns across the agreed dimensions.
8. Highlight what is common, distinctive, risky, or potentially useful.
9. Produce UX/product implications, not just a feature table.
10. End with caveats, confidence, and a clean URL list when URLs were used.

## Example comparison dimensions

- Onboarding path
- Information architecture
- Conversion flow
- Empty/error states
- Trust signals
- Pricing or packaging presentation
- Progressive profiling
- Accessibility and clarity
- Visual hierarchy
- Support or help moments

## Output

Recommended output:

- Brief recap
- Comparator set
- Comparison dimensions
- Pattern summary
- Evidence table
- UX/product implications
- Risks and opportunities
- Recommended next actions
- Clean URL list

## Common failures

- **Feature table without insight.** Add implications for the user's product or
  UX decision.
- **Commentary is treated as product evidence.** Label commentary separately and
  prefer direct product/account evidence.
- **Dimensions change mid-scan.** Define comparison dimensions before collecting
  examples.
