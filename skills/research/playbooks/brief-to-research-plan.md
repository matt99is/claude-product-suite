# Brief To Research Plan

## When to use

Use this when the user asks for research but has not yet made the decision
context, audience, constraints, or expected output clear enough to guide source
selection.

## What Claude needs first

- The subject or question to research.
- What the research must inform.
- Who will use the research.
- Any constraints on geography, sector, date range, source type, or depth.
- The desired output format.

## Brief fields

Capture a lightweight brief with:

- The ask
- Why this matters
- What the research must inform
- Audience or stakeholder context
- Constraints
- Expected output
- Known assumptions or supplied context

## Steps

1. Restate the user's rough ask in plain language.
2. Identify what is missing from the brief.
3. Ask the smallest number of questions needed to target the research.
4. Confirm what the research must inform: a UX decision, product direction,
   opportunity/risk assessment, recommendation, or next artefact.
5. Turn the answers into a short research plan.
6. Name likely source categories and any known weak-source traps.
7. Ask for approval before source collection when scope, stakes, or time cost is
   high.

## Output

Produce:

- Brief recap
- Research questions
- Source strategy
- Output shape
- Constraints and exclusions

## Common failures

- **Brief is too broad.** Ask what decision the research must inform.
- **Output is unclear.** Ask whether the user wants findings, recommendations,
  source notes, competitor comparison, or a paste-ready evidence bundle.
- **Research scope balloons.** Propose a narrower first pass with clear limits.
