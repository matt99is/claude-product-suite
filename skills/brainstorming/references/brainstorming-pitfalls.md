# Brainstorming Pitfalls

Use this reference when a brainstorming request is broad, ambiguous, or close to
another specialist skill.

## Idea soup

Long flat lists feel productive but are hard to use. Group options into themes,
name the useful differences, and identify strongest paths.

## Premature narrowing

If the user asks for ideas, do not jump straight to one recommendation. Generate
enough range first, then converge lightly.

## Evidence laundering

Brainstormed ideas are not validated just because they sound plausible. Label
creative possibilities, assumptions, and product judgment. Route to `research`
when the user needs evidence, best practices, competitor examples, or
source-backed synthesis.

## Over-routing

Do not let brainstorming swallow specialist jobs:

- Source-backed evidence -> `research`.
- Artefact critique -> `design-critique`.
- UserTesting.com study operations -> `usertesting`.
- Figma mutation -> `figma-writing`.

Ask one clarifying question when the user could plausibly want either ideation
or one of those specialist workflows.

## Generic cliches

Avoid vague advice such as "improve onboarding", "make it more personalized",
or "add AI" unless it is made specific to the audience, context, and goal.

## Overbuilding

Do not automatically turn every idea into an MVP, roadmap, implementation spec,
or research plan. Offer those handoffs only when they match the requested next
step.
