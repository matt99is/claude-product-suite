# Brainstorming Quality Bar

Use this reference to keep brainstorming useful rather than merely fluent.

## Good Outputs

Good brainstorming outputs are:

- **Specific**: each option has enough concrete shape to discuss, reject, test,
  or turn into another artefact.
- **Audience-aware**: ideas connect to the user, buyer, stakeholder, or context
  the work is meant to serve.
- **Varied**: options explore meaningfully different strategies, not small
  wording changes around one idea.
- **Constraint-aware**: ideas respect stated timing, channel, platform, team,
  brand, regulatory, or technical constraints.
- **Assumption-labelled**: product judgment and creative interpretation are
  clearly separated from evidence-backed claims.
- **Actionable**: the output ends with strongest directions, next questions, or
  a concrete next step.

## Idea Variety

When generating options, vary at least two dimensions that matter to the ask:

- User problem or motivation.
- Product surface or touchpoint.
- Level of ambition.
- Emotional tone.
- Business objective.
- Risk and confidence.
- Time to learn.

Do not pad a list with near-duplicates. Fewer sharper options are better than a
long list with weak variation.

## Convergence

A good brainstorm should not stop at raw ideas. It should lightly converge by
clustering ideas, naming tradeoffs, and identifying the strongest paths. When
recommending, state what would change the recommendation.

## Evidence Language

Use precise language:

- "Creative possibility" for an unvalidated idea.
- "Assumptions" for things that need checking.
- "Product judgment" for reasoned interpretation.
- "Evidence-backed" only when the user supplied evidence or a research skill
  has produced it.
