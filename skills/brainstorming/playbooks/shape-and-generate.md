# Shape And Generate

Use this playbook for fuzzy ideas, broad ideation, product concepts, UX flow
options, positioning angles, feature shaping, copy directions, and experiment
ideas.

## Intake

Capture only what is needed:

- Goal: what decision or artefact should this help?
- Audience: who is this for?
- Context: product, surface, channel, maturity, or situation.
- Constraints: time, brand, platform, business limits, or must-avoid ideas.
- Desired breadth: a few strong directions or many raw options.

If the request is clear enough, proceed without extra questions.

## Shape Fuzzy Ideas

For a rough idea, make the concept explicit:

- User problem or motivation.
- Proposed value.
- Core interaction or experience.
- Why it might matter.
- Main assumptions.
- Obvious risks or weak points.

Then decide whether to generate options or lightly narrow.

## Generate Options

Create options that differ meaningfully. Useful lenses include:

- Conservative, practical, ambitious.
- User-led, business-led, brand-led.
- Utility, trust, delight, speed, learning.
- First-use, repeat-use, recovery, edge-case moments.
- Low-effort experiment, prototype, full product direction.

Group options into themes when there are more than five. Name why each theme is
interesting and where it may be weak.

## Output

Use this shape unless the user asked for another format:

1. Goal recap.
2. Key assumptions.
3. Option themes.
4. Best individual ideas.
5. Risks or unknowns.
6. Strongest next move.

Do not claim the ideas are validated. If evidence is needed, suggest `research`
as a possible next step.
