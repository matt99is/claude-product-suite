# Compare And Narrow

Use this playbook when the user has known options, wants a recommendation, needs
an MVP slice, or asks what to prioritize first.

## Intake

Confirm or infer:

- Decision to make.
- Options under consideration.
- Audience or stakeholder.
- Constraints.
- Criteria that matter most.

If criteria are missing, choose a small default set and label it as an
assumption.

## Default Criteria

Use only the criteria that fit the ask:

- User value.
- Business value.
- Feasibility.
- Differentiation.
- Risk.
- Confidence.
- Speed to learn.
- Reversibility.

## Compare

For each option, identify:

- Best case.
- Main tradeoff.
- Assumptions.
- Why it might fail.
- What evidence would help.

Prefer clear prose over scoring unless the user asks for a matrix.

## Narrow

Recommend one to three strongest directions. Include:

- Recommended direction.
- Why it wins.
- What to defer.
- What would change the recommendation.
- The next practical step.

If the next step requires evidence, critique, UserTesting.com, or Figma
mutation, offer the relevant handoff without making it mandatory.
